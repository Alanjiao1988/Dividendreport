## 4. Company and Listing Structure

GSK is a UK-domiciled, UK-incorporated pharmaceutical company — an ordinary share, not an H-share, red-chip, REIT, or fund, so none of the special withholding regimes apply.

- **Domicile / issuer type:** UK plc, ordinary commercial pharmaceutical company (Specialty Medicines, Vaccines, General Medicines/HIV via ViiV).
- **Listing venue:** Primary listing London Stock Exchange (ticker GSK, ordinary shares, GBP 0.3125 nominal). Secondary listing as ADR on NYSE (ticker GSK; 1 ADR = 2 ordinary shares).
- **Dividend currency:** GBP pence (declared and paid to LSE holders). ADR holders receive USD, converted by the depositary bank at the prevailing FX rate near each record date — this creates FX variance in the USD dividend actually received even when the pence amount is unchanged.
- **Reporting currency:** GBP.
- **Official share count:** ~4.0 billion ordinary shares before the FY2025–FY2026 buyback; the £2bn buyback programme (Feb 2025–Jun 2026) retired ~124 million shares (~3.1% of shares outstanding), completed June 2026. Precise post-buyback count not independently re-confirmed this session — treat ~3.9bn ordinary shares (~1.95bn ADR-equivalent) as an approximation.
- **Security type:** Ordinary share / ADR. Not a REIT, trust, fund, or partnership — standard dividend-income analysis applies.

## 5. Dividend Treatment

**Withholding 0% — gross equals net.** GSK is a UK plc, and the UK does not impose withholding tax on ordinary dividends paid to non-resident individual shareholders — this applies identically whether held as LSE ordinary shares or as NYSE ADRs, because the ADR's underlying UK-source dividend is not treated as US-source income for US withholding purposes.

- Withholding rate: 0%.

Note: ADR holders may see a small depositary pass-through fee (cents per share, e.g. ~$0.005/ADR), which is an administrative fee, not a tax withholding, and is economically minor.

## 8. Management Capital Allocation

Management has run a clear, sequenced capital-allocation playbook since the 2022 demerger: deleverage and absorb the Zantac liability first, then resume buybacks once the balance sheet and litigation overhang allowed it.

- **Dividend policy:** Stated progressive ordinary dividend policy with an implicit payout-ratio anchor (~40% of core EPS); reaffirmed at FY2025 results alongside 2026 guidance of 70p.
- **Buyback policy:** Deprioritized in 2022–2024 while net debt was reduced and the Zantac settlement reserve was built; £2bn buyback programme announced 5 Feb 2025 (alongside FY2024 results), executed over ~16 months, completed June 2026 — 123.9 million shares repurchased for just under £2bn.
- **Leverage target:** Net debt/core EBITDA has run ~1.2x–1.3x through 2024–2025, investment-grade territory for pharma; the 2025 uptick (1.2x→1.3x) reflects buyback resumption plus residual Zantac cash outflows and business-development activity (e.g., the ~$4bn ViiV-related transaction with Pfizer/Shionogi agreed Jan 2026).
- **Reinvestment priority:** Capex on R&D/intangibles has been rising (2023 £1.0bn → 2025 £1.5bn intangibles spend), consistent with pipeline investment in Specialty Medicines and Vaccines.
- **Acquisition / business development:** Bolt-on and royalty-related transactions (ViiV/Pfizer/Shionogi) rather than large transformative M&A.
- **Share issuance / ATM:** No evidence of equity issuance or ATM activity found; buybacks are a genuine net reduction, not offset by issuance.

## 9. Buyback Quality

- **Share-count change:** Real reduction — 123.9 million ordinary shares (~3.1% of shares outstanding) retired under the £2bn programme, Feb 2025–Jun 2026.
- **Dilution:** No offsetting issuance found; the reduction is genuine.
- **Valuation discipline:** Average repurchase price during the programme (~£14.73–£16 per share in its early tranches) was well below the current ~£18.80 price — buybacks were executed at a materially cheaper valuation than today's price, a genuinely favorable outcome for continuing shareholders, though not evidence of unusually disciplined *timing* (the shares simply re-rated upward afterward).
- **Debt-funded?** No — funded from operating FCF with only a modest, explainable net-debt uptick alongside residual Zantac payments.
- **Offset by issuance?** No.

**Buyback Quality: Good.**
